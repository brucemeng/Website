With the holidays being so near, I got curious as to whether there were any differences in spending patterns throughout the year for Canadians.

I pulled together some data that I had on hand, combined with some additional data from Statistics Canada. Here is what it looks like:

ggplot(spend, aes(x = avg.s, y = Month, fill = ..x..)) +
        geom_density_ridges_gradient(scale = 2, rel_min_height = 0.026, gradient_lwd = 1.,
                                     color = "black") +
        scale_fill_viridis(name = "Daily Spend", option = "magma") +
        xlab("Average Daily Spending") +
        scale_x_continuous(labels = dollar_format(), expand = c(0.01,0)) +
        theme_ridges() +
        ggtitle("Daily spending, per-person, by consumers in Canada") +
        ylab(NULL) +
        guides(fill = F)
## Picking joint bandwidth of 3.47

So it definitely looks like December has the highest daily spend amount. It also looks like most Canadians pull back on spending in the three months after Christmas, until about May, when spending increases once again. My guess is that this is probably to prepare for the coming spring/summer with vacations and new wardrobes, but to be honest your guess is as good as mine.
